--- layout: blog section-type: blog title: Blog sitemap: priority: 1.0 --- # 23rd February 2022 I feel alone. I read here and I am sad because I don't have this, an overlapping of friendship and interests: Angelo Vulpiani, professore di fisica teorica all'università La Sapienza di Roma, ha raccontato quando nel febbraio del 1980 rischiò di essere arrestato insieme a Roberto Benzi. "All’epoca potevamo usare solo nelle ore notturne un computer dell’Istituto di Fisica dell’Atmosfera del CNR; dopo aver lanciato la simulazione uscivamo, verso le 3 di notte, per comprare i cornetti. Una volta venimmo circondati da una decina di poliziotti: c’era una riunione notturna della Democrazia Cristiana con mezzo governo. Ci salvò la lettera del Direttore dell’Istituto che ci autorizzava a entrare di notte, e forse anche la nostra aria svagata: due giovani che di notte andavano in giro parlando di equazioni differenziali stocastiche e mangiando cornetti alla crema non potevano essere pericolosi." Following what I wrote yesterday, I don't want proselytes, I don't want friends, I don't want mentors and I don't want ideals. I have them. I want peers, without trumping the self-transcending ideal. I want people that worship the same things that I worship. But life goes so fast, I can barely exchange notes with people intersecting my road coming from another place and going who knows where. I should listen more to my guts, as an italian song goes: "art is thought leaving the body, no more no less like shit". I shit to survive. I am creative to survive. Grigori Perelman is clearly an insane person, my point is that he is not alone. It's not just the isolation and sickness coming from having Covid speaking here. The problem of being faster than light is that you can only live in darkness. And I don't think I am that successful in pushing my limits, so there is no glory in such loneliness.
